Career

Gervonta Davis made his professional debut against Desi Williams on February 22, 2013, at the age of 18 years old. Gervonta won the fight via first-round knockout. In addition, on February 20, 2015, at the CONSOL Energy Center in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania, he became the first person to knock out Puerto Rico’s Israel Suarez, knocking him out in the first round.

On May 22, 2015, at The Claridge Hotel in Atlantic City, New Jersey, he defeated Alberto Mora of Mexico via technical knockout 1 minute, 14 seconds into the fight. Furthermore, on September 12, 2015, Gervonta defeated Recky Dulay (8–1, 5 KOs) of the Philippines in only 94 seconds at the MGM Grand Garden Arena in Las Vegas on Showtime as part of the Floyd Mayweather vs. Andre Berto undercard.

On Spike TV on April 1, 2016, he knocked out Guillermo Avila in the sixth round at the D.C. Armory. On November 15, 2016, ESPN announced that he would fight for the IBF junior lightweight title against undefeated Jos Pedraza. He defeated Pedraza in the seventh round to win the IBF junior lightweight title. On May 7, 2017, it is announced that he will defend his championship against Liam Walsh in London, England. The IGF has ranked Liam first. He stopped Walsh in the third round to retain his IBF title.

On August 10, Ringtv announced that Gervonta would face unbeaten prospect Francisco Fonseca, who was ranked seventh by the IBF at the time, in a fight that he would win by knockout in round eight. Furthermore, he won for the tenth time in a row by knocking out his opponent. He’d most likely face Jesus Cuellar, a former featherweight champion, on February 21. In front of 13,964 people, he knocked out Cuellar in round three. He announced in November 2018 that he would defend his WBA title against former three-weight world champion Abner Mares in February 2019 in Southern California.

The fight will air on Showtime on February 9, 2019, at the Pechanga Arena in San Diego, it was announced on December 14. Pechanga and Gervonta never fought on the scheduled date; instead, he faced Hugo Ruiz, whom he knocked out in the first round after breaking his opponent’s nose. On July 27, 2019, he defended his WBA Super-Featherweight Championship against Ricardo Nunez.

On December 28, 2019, he won the vacant WBA (Regular) lightweight championship by twelfth-round technical knockout over former unified featherweight world champion Yuriorkis Gamboa. Yuriorkis rose to second in the WBA’s lightweight division at the time. He made his Showtime PPV debut on October 31, 2020, when he defended his lightweight title against four-division world champion Léo Santa Cruz at The Alamodome in San Antonio, Texas.

Furthermore, in round 6, he landed a hard left uppercut on Santa Cruz, who immediately fell to the canvas. The fight was stopped by the referee, and he retained his lightweight title while capturing Santa Cruz’s WBA (Super) super featherweight title.

For the first time in his career, he will compete as the main event of a Showtime PPV on June 26, 2021, at the State Farm Arena in Atlanta, Georgia, against undefeated WBA (Regular) champion Mario Barrios. On the night, he won a competitive fight, knocking his opponent down twice in the eighth and again in the eleventh rounds en route to a technical knockout victory in the eleventh round.
