Sara Porter On Secret Restoration

Secret Restoration of History Channel is a reality TV series that followed a team of some of the nation’s most skilled and knowledgeable restorers and craftsmen who work together to fulfill people’s aspirations. As the title suggests, these experts repair and restore some significant family treasures and other priceless items in a secret facility. After giving their best effort, the artisans show the repaired things to unaware customers.

The artists in this reality program work with a variety of tools, including vintage pinball machines, hot rods, guitars, and ancient canons. The unique idea makes it a wholesome and informative viewing, with the restorers revealing secret elements of the ancient objects they work on and giving people some prized possessions.

One of the cast members of the reality show is Sara Porter. According to her bio, “she founded S. Porter Restoration & Conservation LLC. She has traveled the globe studying the world of materials and techniques from her studio in NYC to Africa to Japan. For over 25 years, her insistence on using only traditional materials has made her a favorite of art and antique dealers, interior designers, private collectors, and museums.”

She is widely known for her ability to perfectly match the color on pieces dating from the 20th Century as far back as 200 BC.

Sara’s forte includes “Restoration / Conservation / Reproduction of English & Asian Art & Antiquities including Lacquer, Japanning, Chinoiserie, Leather & Paper Screens, Antique Painted Finishes, Eglomise, Water & Oil-Size Gilding. Color matching specialty.”

Secret Restoration was reportedly filmed in Connecticut, in Bridgeport, Stamford, and Southington. The filming began in April 2022 and wrapped up in July. Other cast members joining her in the show are; Leah Bolden, PJ Burchett, David Dall, Alfred Dill, and LaTasha “LA” Thym.

Before the show’s premiere, Sara announced on her Facebook, “Been trying to find a pic which really shows how much brighter the entire boat including sails are post Restoration/Conservation. On its journey through life, someone had sprayed the entire thing down with copper-colored spray paint. Reversing that choice took some solid time!”

Sara Porter Net Worth

Sara Porter’s net worth in 2022 exceeds $450 thousand. She started her career in 1989 working at Fine Arts Conservation Inc. She worked as an employee there till 1993. Three years later, she joined S. Porter Restoration & Conservation LLC. She ran the company for 15 years till 2015.

S. Porter Restoration & Conservation LLC has been one of the most unique and respected art & antique repair studios in the industry. Founder Sara Porter brings over two decades of extensive hands-on experience in lacquer, japanning, leather & paper screens, painted finishes, eglomise, water & oil-size gilding, and color-matching to virtually every level of English & Asian fine art and antiquities.

S. Porter Restoration & Conservation LLC has full studios in Long Island City and the Hudson Valley, with an experienced on-site team available for location projects.

Sara has studied Painting and Sculpture at Syracuse University; painting at Rhode Island School of Design; tile and mold-making at Parsons School Of Design; ceramics and fiber at Parsons’ Ivory Coast/Africa program; and chinoiserie & urushi in Tokyo, Nara, and Kyoto, Japan.

After working for many years as a restorer, conservator, and consultant with several prestigious metropolitan firms, Sara launched her own company to focus on rare international art and furnishings with one-of-a-kind restoration and conservation challenges.
