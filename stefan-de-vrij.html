Stefan de Vrij Biography

Stefan De Vrij was born on February 5, 1992. Jan de Vrij, his father, is a former professional football player who played for the local football team VV Spirit. He was raised with his elder brothers, Niels and Eric. They both grew up playing football. De Vrij joined VV Spirit at the age of five and then went to Feyenoord in 2002.

Career

  • Stefan De Vrij signed his first professional deal with Feyenoord on July 17, 2009. On September 24, 2009, he made his first-team debut in a 0-5 away victory over Harkemase in the KNVB Cup.
  • Similarly, De Vrij made his Eredivisie debut on December 6, 2009, against FC Groningen, which he won 3-1.
  • From 2009 to 2014, he made 135 appearances for Feyenoord, scoring 7 goals.
  • On July 30, 2014, the player signed a deal with Serie A club Lazio for an unknown amount. On August 24, he made his debut for the club in the third round of the “Coppa Italian” against Bassano Virtus, winning 7-0 at home. Vrij made his Serie A debut a week later in a 3-1 loss to Inter.
  • He appeared in 71 games for the club, wearing number three, and scored five goals. In 2018, he departed the club.
  • De Vrij joined Inter Milan in July 2018 and agreed to a five-year deal.
    International Career of Stefan de Vrij
  • De Vrij made his Netherlands U16 National Team debut on November 21, 2007, in a friendly match against Ukraine, which resulted in a 1-0 victory. He also played for the Netherlands U-17 team at the “2009 UEFA European U-17 Football Championship” in Germany.
  • He was also a part of the U-17 squad that finished second in the European Championship and qualified for the “2009 FIFA U-17 World Cup” in Nigeria.
  • On May 7, 2012, De Vrij was called up for the first time for the “UEFA Euro 2012” competition. On August 15, 2015, he made his international debut in a friendly match against Belgium, which he lost 4-2.
  • He was also named as a defender for the “2014 FIFA World Cup” with Jasper Cillessen, Arjen Robben, and Bruno Martins Indi. His first goal came on June 13, 2014, in a 5-1 victory against Spain at Arena Fonte Nova in Salvador, Brazil.
