Who is Jermaine Hopkins?

Jermaine Hopkins is an American actor. He is best known for his roles as Thomas Sams in the movie Lean on Me and as Steel in the movie Juice.

He has appeared in four episodes of the TV film The Royal Family (1991-1992).

Age, Parents, Siblings, Family, Ethnicity, Nationality

Jermaine Hopkins was born on 23 August 1973, in Newark, New Jersey, USA. His nationality is American and he is of African-American ethnicity.

Jermaine Hopkins: Professional Life and Career

Jermaine Hopkins made his acting debut at the age of 14, playing the role of a misguided drug-abusing high school student, Thomas Sams in the film Lean on Me (1989).

After that, he had the starring role as Steel in the 1992 American crime drama film Juice. He also had a role as Buddy in the TV film The Royal Family (1991-1992).  He also had roles in the movies such as Strapped, Murder One, The Parent ‘Hood, and Moesha.

Hopkins played the role of Dupree in the TV series The Wayans Bros (1996-1998). Jermaine Hopkins completed ten episodes of the series.

He also guests starred in an episode of the television series, Law & Order: Special Victims Unit with Mariska Hargitay. In 2002, he appeared as Chronic in the film Urban Massacre.
