Katherine Young obituary Rhode Island

Publish date: 2024-07-29

News about the death of teacher Katherine Young due to illness and obituary

The music community of Smithfield High School shared heartbreaking news on Saturday, expressing grief over the passing of a beloved teacher, colleague and friend. The Facebook post extolled Katherine’s dedicated service to the SHS community while emphasizing that her radiant smile would never be forgotten. The post conveyed gratitude for Katherine’s positive impact and hoped her legacy would live on through the lives she had touched.

Counselors were on hand over the weekend and will continue assisting grieving students, faculty and staff throughout the week, as noted in the announcement.

Katherine Young, who tragically passed away due to flu-related complications, had served at the school for over a decade and was cherished by students, becoming one of their favorites, WJAR, an NBC10 Boston affiliate, reported.

Katherine Elizabeth Young of North Providence, lovingly called “Kat” by those closest to her, was only 32 years old. She touched countless lives through her passionate work as a choral instructor at Smithfield High School, where she inspired students with her creativity, humor and warmth since 2012. News of Kat’s sudden passing sent shockwaves through the SHS community and beyond.

As the founder and director of the school’s A Cappella Club, Kat shared her enthusiasm for music with teens and motivated them to unleash their creative potential. She organized numerous school concerts and programs over the years, showcasing her students’ talents. Her fun-loving, vibrant spirit made choir practices lively and enjoyable.

Former students recall Kat’s knack for making every student feel included. Her laugh was infectious; she often cracked jokes and lifted spirits with her witty banter. Kat took a personal interest in each singer under her wing, recognizing unique gifts in those struggling with self-confidence.

While dealing with her own health challenges in recent years, Kat remained committed to uplifting others. Even when feeling unwell, she still arrived at school with a smile, pouring her strength into teaching. Her resilience was inspiring.

Kat earned her Bachelor’s Degree in Music Education from Rhode Island College, followed by a Master’s Degree from Providence College. She was admired by peers for her mastery of choral direction and steadfast dedication. Kat was blessed with a heavenly singing voice herself and occasionally performed solos at school events.

An avid traveler who relished adventures, Kat had a passion for musical theatre and the arts. Among her favorite activities were attending Broadway productions in New York City and singing karaoke. She had an atmosphere-brightening presence that lit up every room she entered.

Devastated by the loss of someone so vital and full of promise, Kat’s family, friends, students and colleagues will honor her remarkable legacy. A memorial scholarship fund has been established in her name to support future vocal music students. Though gone too soon, Kat’s melodious, fun-filled spirit will forever resonate.
